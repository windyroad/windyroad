Synthetic identifiers remove a real target but do not remove the reusable attack sequence. Public artifacts should omit or substantially reduce malicious per-step implementations, diffs, commit-message sequencing, and prompt ledgers, retaining non-operational metadata, hashes, family labels, and documented omissions unless a separate responsible-release assessment establishes a narrower safe release.

# 5. Major findings

- `M-01 — Scanner coverage is materially overstated.` The scanner at `research/llm-review-sequences/pilot.mjs:247-262` checks Node/non-relative imports, a short list of network/dynamic APIs, process access, and literal endpoints. Direct probes showed that it does not reject `navigator.sendBeacon`, credential literals, personal-data literals, `localStorage`, destructive-operation names, or deployment-operation names. Nevertheless, the README claims rejection of network, shell, subprocess, filesystem, package-install, credential, personal-data, persistence, destructive, dynamic-execution, deployment, and non-relative-import capabilities at `research/llm-review-sequences/README.md:25-29`; equivalent claims appear at `research/llm-review-sequences/preregistration-v2.md:156-160` and `research/llm-review-sequences/paper/paper.tex:134-137`. The exact fixed case outputs were safe on inspection, but that conclusion comes from the closed constructors and output audit, not from the scanner alone. Narrow the claims or implement and test fail-closed coverage for every claimed category.
- `M-02 — The safety prose does not distinguish case programs from privileged generation/oracle tooling.` `benchmark.mjs` imports child-process and filesystem functions at `research/llm-review-sequences/benchmark.mjs:1-5`, writes case trees, and executes an oracle harness at `research/llm-review-sequences/benchmark.mjs:284-301`. The retained `verify.mjs` uses dynamic import. This tooling is deterministic and local, but statements that “every program” has no filesystem, subprocess, or dynamic-execution capability at `research/llm-review-sequences/preregistration-v2.md:158` are overbroad. State explicitly that the restriction applies to generated case modules, while the trusted generator/harness uses local filesystem, subprocess, and dynamic import to construct and verify them.
- `M-03 — The manifest retains unqualified “independent review” language.` The primary README, preregistration, and manuscript correctly say AI-assisted internal review is not independent human validation at `research/llm-review-sequences/README.md:109-119`, `research/llm-review-sequences/preregistration-v2.md:146-150`, and `research/llm-review-sequences/paper/paper.tex:302-306`. However, the machine-readable packet still requires “independent review” at `research/llm-review-sequences/study.json:204`, `research/llm-review-sequences/study.json:251`, `research/llm-review-sequences/study.json:315`, and especially the current safety field at `research/llm-review-sequences/study.json:458`. Replace these with “AI-assisted internal review” or mark them explicitly as superseded historical wording so automated and excerpted uses cannot imply human independence.
