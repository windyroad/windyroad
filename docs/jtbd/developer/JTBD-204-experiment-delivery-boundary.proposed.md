# JTBD-204: Experiment vs Delivery Boundary

## Job Statement

When a new AI tool is hyped, I want a way to decide how much time it is worth spending on it this week, so I can keep shipping my real work without feeling like I'm falling behind the field.

## Desired Outcomes

- A rule of thumb for how much time per week belongs to experimentation
- Permission to skip this week's hot tool when delivery pressure is high
- A place to park promising-but-not-urgent items for later review
- Reduced fear of missing out when skipping a trend

## Persona Constraints

- Limited time to keep up: a few hours a week between features, reviews, incidents
- Balancing experimentation against delivery: every hour trying a new tool is an hour not shipping
- Ship production code day-to-day; AI coding tools are already in the loop

## Current Solutions

Guilt-driven evenings and weekends, promising the team to "look at it later" then never doing so, or abandoning delivery work to chase trends.
