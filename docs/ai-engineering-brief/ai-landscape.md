## Analysis

### Differentiation

Shipped Product is supported by two high-visibility dependencies on the map: Engineering Team Capability and Delivery Pipeline. Both are what the reader's organisation pays for; everything lower on the map is substrate that feeds them. This matters because an AI Coding Assistants choice is effectively an Engineering Team Capability decision, and a Delivery Pipeline choice determines where AI Security Tooling hooks in. Read the custom components below with that chain in mind: what looks like deep-substrate tooling shapes the two top-visibility components the business depends on.

Agentic Engineering Practices sit at Genesis (0.15) with high visibility (0.78), AI Security Tooling has just crossed into early Custom-Built (0.18, shifted from 0.16 this week as OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5-Cyber and Trusted Access for Cyber on May 7 (the first tier-1 vertical-cyber frontier model), the UK AISI's May 13 paper found autonomous AI cyber capability "doubling every few months", and Google and NYT confirmed criminal hackers used AI to find a major software flaw on May 11; the y-axis move puts the component just past the 0.17 Custom-Built threshold), Model Evaluation Frameworks at early Custom-Built (0.28, holding this week as the AISI doubling framing reinforces last week's trajectory without yet moving the position further; evaluation frameworks now need capability-tier mapping to threat-model thresholds, not just benchmark scores), AI Regulation and Compliance (0.32, holding this week against regional divergence: US federal action stalled per Axios with Mayorkas endorsing voluntary policies and a Silicon Valley lobbying blitz per NYT, while Spain pushes ahead, the UK AISI continues evaluations, and Apple criticises EU measures), Engineering Team Capability (0.32, shifted from 0.30 this week as OpenAI's Deployment Company launch on May 11 makes vendor-as-services-partner a two-lab industry pattern alongside last week's Anthropic-Blackstone-Hellman & Friedman-Goldman Sachs joint venture, with Anthropic's Claude for Small Business and legal-tools expansion widening the surface), and Prompt and Context Management (0.30, holding) all sit in the Custom-Built band or below. AI Agent Runtimes sits in the Product band at 0.40, having crossed last week on a cluster of vertical-specific agent product launches (Anthropic finance agents, OpenAI/PwC for the CFO office, DeepMind co-clinician); its downstream dependencies on Foundation Models, Model Hosting and Inference, Prompt and Context Management, and Model Evaluation Frameworks now run from a Product-band substrate into Custom-Built-or-below substrates, which is exactly the configuration that makes the runtime decision both unreversible and load-bearing for everything underneath it. ### Risk

AI-driven offensive cyber capability is now production-real (new framing this week): the UK AISI reported autonomous AI cyber capability is "doubling every few months" (May 13), OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5-Cyber as a vertical security-specific frontier model (May 7), Google and NYT confirmed criminal hackers used AI to find a major software flaw (May 11), and Axios reported a major cybersecurity firm finding new AI models uncovered 7x more flaws (May 13); the trigger to watch is any in-house disclosure of an AI-found exploit on a system the team operates, and the measurable lead indicator is whether the team's threat model includes AI-accelerated reconnaissance and vulnerability discovery as a baseline assumption rather than an emerging risk. External vendor exposure: when a dominant coding-assistant vendor changes its default model with less than 30 days notice, or moves a previously-included model behind a higher pricing tier, integrations using vendor-default-model assumptions break within one release cycle, and the fallback is an abstraction layer in front of the assistant before consolidation arrives. Hyperscaler distribution edge: with OpenAI models, Codex, and Managed Agents now on AWS, the assistant-vendor-and-runtime-vendor combination has split into a three-way decision (vendor, distribution channel, hyperscaler), which means a contract change at the hyperscaler tier (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure) can now ripple through to assistant pricing without the assistant vendor changing its own terms; the trigger is any hyperscaler announcing a margin or pricing change to its AI-services line. Commodity layer: Model Hosting and Inference (Commodity, 0.70) could change terms; trigger is a hosting provider announcing a deprecation or a pricing change above 20%, affected components are AI Coding Assistants and AI Agent Runtimes, and the fallback is a multi-provider abstraction paired with a contract termination review.
