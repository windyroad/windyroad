# 10. Team voice positioning

Date: 2026-04-13

## Status

Proposed

## Context

Windy Road Technology has been positioning as a solo consulting practice with first-person singular voice ("I help", "I partner", "I audit") across the website, blog, and social media. The `docs/VOICE-AND-TONE.md` guide explicitly states: "The site is a solo consulting practice. The voice is first-person."

As the business pivots to "patch fitness" consulting (LSM canvas v3), the positioning needs to shift. The service offering involves team-scale delivery (embedded delivery leads, delivery sprints) that is better represented by a team voice. Tom Howard remains the named founder and public face, but the work is done by Windy Road as an entity.

This also introduces an "external threat" tone register for communicating about real, verifiable external developments (e.g. AI-powered vulnerability discovery via Claude Mythos) without manufacturing urgency.

## Decision

1. Change the voice from first-person singular ("I") to first-person plural ("we") across all new copy
2. Tom Howard is introduced by name in credentials sections as the founder, but service copy uses "we help", "our team", "we've built"
3. Existing blog articles retain their original voice (first-person singular is fine for practitioner accounts)
4. Add an "external threat" tone register: when referencing real external developments, lead with the visitor's exposure, not the technology's capabilities. State facts. Do not alarm for its own sake.
5. Existing first-person-singular copy on `/founders` and `/vibe-code-audit` is not immediately rewritten but will be updated when those pages are next modified

## Consequences

- All new website copy, landing pages, and service descriptions use "we"
- Blog articles can still use "I" (practitioner sharing personal experience)
- The `docs/VOICE-AND-TONE.md` guide must be updated before any new copy is written
- Social media posts shift to "we" for service-related content, "I" remains acceptable for personal observations
- The external threat register enables Mythos-related messaging without violating the existing "don't manufacture urgency" CTA rule
