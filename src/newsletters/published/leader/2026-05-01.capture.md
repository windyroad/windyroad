## Item 2 (final): AI non-determinism + avoid test theatre

- **Outcome**: Adjust
- **Original take presented**: "An engineer asked an AI to count carbs 27,000 times and got 27,000 different answers. Action this quarter: add a 'consistency at N runs' check to any AI-feature acceptance test."
- **Source**: https://www.diabettech.com/i-asked-ai-to-count-my-carbs-27000-times-it-couldnt-give-me-the-same-answer-twice/ (via HN)
- **Adjust text**:

  > Agree. You need to test the distribution. What are the observed minimums, maximums, averages and quartiles. Are they acceptable. There is a strong chance that this will become a lot like performance testing, and I don't mean that in a good way. Many outfits do performance testing poorly, they run the tests, analyse the results and convince everyone it's a pass, rather than specifying the requirements (and having a legitimate reason for those requirements) and testing against those. I suspect AI testing will become similar, where they will run the tests, analyse the results and convince everyone it's a pass. Avoid this test theatre. Start working on the processes now for how you would define the distribution requirements based on business needs, this takes very capably BAs to extract that information.
