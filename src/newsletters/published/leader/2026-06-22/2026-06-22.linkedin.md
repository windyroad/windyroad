The model was the easy part this week.

Every constraint that limited what a leader could do with AI sat somewhere else this week. A government is negotiating whether your vendor may serve you. The public has soured on the word "AI". And early data on what heavy AI use does to a team's skills is not encouraging.

The hard part of AI is no longer the model (The Shift, Issue 10):

- The US off-switch on Anthropic's top models turned into a negotiation. Talks moved toward standing AI-security rules, the President softened his line, and JPMorgan cut Claude access for its Hong Kong staff. Your model's availability is now a government deal that can swing either way.
- Public sentiment turned measurably negative. About 60% of US consumers say "AI" in brand messaging is a turnoff, and only 16% think AI will help society. The label flipped from tailwind to headwind: sell the result, keep the AI quiet.
- The hedge against that off-switch got stronger. Two weeks after Google's lighter Gemma 4, Z.ai shipped GLM-5.2, now the top-ranked open-weight text model. A model you host yourself is both a cost hedge and a sovereignty hedge: nobody can switch it off.

On skills, the useful question is not how to stop the erosion. It is which skills are going obsolete, chalk-on-slate, and which new ones (architectural and product judgment, not faster code review) you now build on purpose.

If the model is the easy part now, what is the hard part for your team this quarter? Reply and tell us.
