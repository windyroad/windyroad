**Discipline is your AI lever this quarter.**

The AI bottleneck moved off the model. The surfaces that matter this quarter are procurement, narrative survival, security patching, cost discipline, and skill formation.

This week sharpened the case:

• Anthropic's Project Glasswing reached roughly 150 organisations across 15-plus countries while the same company called for AI non-proliferation and filed S-1 paperwork to go public. Scale and caution from the same provider, in the same week.

• Apollo wrapped its $35 billion debt deal to buy Google chips for Anthropic, first reported at $36 billion in late May (Bloomberg). Vendor financial-health checks now belong in your next procurement review beside capability and pricing.

• Uber capped per-developer AI tool spend at $1,500 per month. Pair the cap with a shipped-outcome metric (pull requests merged per engineer per week, feature ship cadence, or mean time to resolve incidents) before next quarter's budget review forces the conversation.

The right response to the new bottlenecks (PR review, security patching, dependency gates) is not to refuse AI work the way the Ladybird browser project did this week. It is to build AI guardrails that triage AI pull requests at AI speed, while staying compliant with the regs that apply to your org.

Discipline as the deliberate cadence at which your team's processes upgrade. Not bureaucracy that slows them down.
