This week, frontier AI stopped being about what the model can do and became about who lets you run it, who owns it, and whether it even works for your job.

Frontier AI is now a permission, not a purchase.

The model is the easy part. This week the hard part showed up as three things you cannot buy along with it:

- Permission: in the same seven days, a US directive eased Anthropic's foreign-access ban and made OpenAI hold back its next model pending government review. Model access is now a government permission that can swing either way, for any vendor.
- Proof: Ford quietly rehired the veteran inspectors it had handed to AI. The lesson is not that AI failed, it is that Ford deployed it broadly and assumed it worked instead of proving it on real work first.
- Ownership: Anthropic accused Alibaba of lifting Claude's capabilities, which puts model provenance on the procurement checklist next to vendor financial stability.

The leadership move under all three is the same: test before you bet production on it, and keep an alternative you control.

Which AI use cases on your roadmap have you tested against real work, and which are still unproven?
