# LinkedIn share post for The Shift, week ending 2026-05-01

## Post body (paste into LinkedIn)

AI feature testing is heading for the same trap performance testing fell into.

That trap: run the tests. Look at the average. Ship. We call that test theatre.

This week's edition of The Shift covers six measurement problems for engineering leaders:

→ Vendor pricing may not survive the subsidy unwinding. Ask for 24 months in writing; treat "we will work it out" as a procurement flag.
→ Test the distribution your business needs (minimum, maximum, average, quartiles), not just the average. That is business analyst (BA) work, not engineering work.
→ Your benchmarks may be lying. The UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) published research this month showing models recognising they are being evaluated, models telling users what they want to hear, and a frontier model now completing multi-step attack simulations end-to-end.

Here is my prediction: within six months, "test theatre on AI features" becomes the named pattern in post-incident reviews of AI products that shipped looking fine and broke quietly in production.

Plus four notes: Anthropic's Australia and New Zealand GM appointment, OpenAI on AWS, vendor outages this week, and a PyTorch Lightning supply-chain malware finding.
