## LinkedIn Post

The UK AI Safety Institute just confirmed a frontier model can run a full cyber-attack. Update your threat model.

This week the UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) confirmed GPT-5.5 can run a multi-step cyber-attack from start to finish. It is the second model to clear that bar; Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview was the first. AISI also published the success rates: GPT-5.5 cleared one named challenge in 10 minutes 22 seconds at a cost of $1.73.

The Thoughtworks Technology Radar Vol 34 landed alongside, with five AI-engineering practices in Adopt and "agent exfiltration risks" in Caution. Two trusted bodies, same week, named maturity tiers in adjacent parts of AI security.

What to act on this week:

- Update your threat model to AISI's tier. The threat is attackers using a frontier model at this tier against your perimeter. Confirm your last red-team exercise reflected that capability. Audit detection-and-response playbooks against multi-step LLM-driven attack chains.

- Score your team against the Radar's Adopt list (Spec-driven development, Zero trust architecture, DORA metrics, mutation testing, measuring collaboration quality with coding agents). Add "agent exfiltration risks" as a row on the AI-vendor risk register.

- Audit endpoints for vendor-installed AI you didn't deploy. Chrome is loading a 4GB Gemini Nano model on user devices without consent (per Tom's Hardware, PCWorld, Decrypt, gHacks). For Chrome-managed fleets, "what AI is on our devices" is now a different question to "what AI did we deploy".

What capability tier does your current threat model assume? Reply with your answer.
