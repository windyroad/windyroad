## LinkedIn Post

AI cyber capability shipped in production this week. So did the AI counter-tools from the same vendors.

Issue 05 of The Shift is out. Three stories. One shape: new vendor capability, vendor counter-tools (the security products the frontier AI labs are now shipping), and how procurement and reward systems decide which lands in your team.

The capability side. OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5-Cyber, the UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) measured autonomous cyber capability as "doubling every few months", and Google plus the New York Times confirmed criminal hackers used AI to develop a major software flaw. All in one seven-day window. Even with humans still load-bearing in defence (Axios, May 14), if your team owns threat modelling, the capability curve has moved since January. A review sized in January is sized against a different curve.

Procurement just became a services-arm decision. OpenAI launched its Deployment Company. That makes two tier-1 labs in two weeks launching a services arm, after Anthropic's enterprise AI services joint venture with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs. AI procurement is no longer "which model" but "which vendor's services arm".

Token-usage leaderboards are a workaround for AI experimentation. Better levers exist. Amazon employees "tokenmaxxing" (gaming internal AI-usage targets) shows the failure mode in the open. Our read is that organisations are using volume metrics as a workaround to encourage experimentation. The intent is right; the mechanism is wrong. Reward learning outcomes, not token volume. Reward plugins others adopt, not wrappers.

What does your team's AI-usage metric actually reward today?
