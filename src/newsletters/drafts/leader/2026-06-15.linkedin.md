## LinkedIn Post

A government switched two frontier AI models off for foreign users overnight this week, and a competitor's lobbying was reportedly part of how it happened. That changes a question every engineering leader should be asking.

Own your AI supply before someone else switches it off (The Shift, Issue 09):

- Model access is now a continuity risk, not just a vendor SLA. Put a self-hostable open-weight model in your evaluation set now. Google's Gemma 4, open and non-Chinese, is where I would start.
- The headcount calculus is not a clean substitution. AI lifts the capacity ceiling that limits what you can build, and that surfaced demand is often the case for more capacity, not less.
- The rules that bind you are fragmenting. State AGs are investigating OpenAI while the federal government tries to preempt state law. Do not write contracts that assume one standard is arriving.
- AI-enabled attack is now a named, framework-mapped class (Anthropic and MITRE mapped a year of it to ATT&CK). Ask your security team one question: are we testing against these techniques?
